Professional Experience

Cardiovascular flow analysis with MTV

Jun. 2021 – Present : Over the last two summers, I have assisted Dr. Kartik Bulusu with cardiovascular research and implementing the MTV experimental technique to extract high-precision fluid flow data from a human artery model. I made contributions through a review of recent relevant literature and the preparation and execution of experiments. In October 2022, in collaboration with Dr. Charles Fort and his group, we collected initial experimental data on the subject of MTV to Measure Wall Shear Stress in Model Cardiovascular Flows. I had an opportunity to attend APS 2022 Fluid Dynamics conference in Indianapolis, IN, where we presented our progress.


Delivery drone charging system

Jan. 2022 – Present : In 2022, I had an opportunity to intern at a start-up, Drone Point Solutions, LLC., to design a drone charging system specifically designed for package delivery drones. I researched innovations and standard practices regarding battery charging and energy management. Relevant topics also included the configuration of EV charging systems and cars. As a result, I learned about battery management techniques, battery technology, and charging systems.



Other Experience

New Venture Competition

Feb. - Apr. 2023: The George Washington University’s New Venture Competition is the #1 largest collegiate competition in the country. Our team, SecuFoam, has made a first-round submission and is competing in the Health & Life Sciences track (as of 01/29/2023). The IoT-based product SecuFoam is meant to equip large institutions with tools to prevent and track the spread of common illnesses. As a team of multidisciplinary engineers, we are excited for an opportunity to put forth a venture proposal and make a contribution to public health.
